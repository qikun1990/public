    Day 29: Verifying Tracking Etc.

    ***********************************

    ACTION & EXPLANATION

    In the previous lesson, we've set up a campaign on either PropellerAds or PopAds, and traffic should be running. In the next couple of lessons, we want to look at the data gathered, and learn how to do TESTING and OPTIMIZATION accordingly.

    But before we proceed, there are a few things we need to do, that I want to get out of the way first - which is the purpose of this lesson.



    *****

    Verify Traffic is Going to the Right Offer(s)

    First, we need to verify that the traffic is going to the intended offers! Here's how to check, for each affiliate network.


    1)If at least one of the offers you're running is from Mobidea:

    -Log into Mobidea, click on "Stats & Tracker" in the left sidebar.

    -Set the time period dropdown at the top, to include the entire time the campaign has been running.

    -Click on "Offer" and select "Offer" from the dropdown. Wait for stats to load.

    -In the stats that are shown, you should see the offer you've been sending traffic to, and some numbers in the "VISITS" and "CLICKS" columns.



    -Make sure the offer(s) listed in the report is/are the one(s) you've chosen to run! If you see one or more offers that you didn't add to the tracker to sent traffic to, it means:

    a)You've set up the campaign incorrectly on the tracker - either made a mistake when entering the offer url into the tracker, or selected the wrong offer when creating the campaign in the tracker, or set up tracker rules incorrectly such that you're sending unaccepted traffic to one or more offers (e.g. sending IOS traffic to an offer that only accepts Android).

    AND/OR

    b)You've set up wrong targeting at the traffic source, such that you're sending unaccepted traffic to one or more offers.

    AND/OR

    c)There's something wrong with the way the affiliate network is redirecting the traffic - e.g. the offer you were intending to run has expired, or has reached offer cap (some offers have a quota on how many conversions can be made every 24 hours). In this case you'd need to check with your AM.

    Whichever the case may be - if you've verified your tracker campaign settings and traffic source campaign settings, and checked with your AM, and STILL can't pinpoint the issue - feel free to start a thread in the forum to describe the exact situation, and we'll help you do further troubleshooting.


    2)If at least one of the offers you're running is from Clickdealer:

    -Log into Clickdealer, you should be at the Dashboard, but if not, click on "Dashboard" on the left sidebar.

    -Set the time period dropdown at the top, to include the entire time the campaign has been running. Click "Apply".



    -Scroll to the bottom and you should see offer stats:



    -Again, make sure the offer(s) listed in the report is/are the one(s) you've chosen to run. (We've covered what to do if this is not the case - see the Mobidea section above.)


    3)If at least one of the offers you're running is from Gotzha:

    -Log into Gotzha, you should be at the Dashboard, but if not, click on "Dashboard" in the left sidebar.

    -Set the "Start" and "End" dropdowns at the top, to include the entire time the campaign has been running. Click "Filter". You should see a summary of offers stats:



    -Again, make sure the offer(s) listed in the report is/are the one(s) you've chosen to run. (We've covered what to do if this is not the case - see the Mobidea section above.)



    *****

    Verify Your Conversion Postback Was Set Up Correctly

    Above, we've covered how to look for offer stats at each affiliate network. Check those same stats to see whether any of the offers you're running has made a conversion (in the "Conversion" or "Signups" column).

    At the time you're reading this, your campaign may or may not have made it's first conversion. If it hasn't, then just keep checking your aff network offer stats periodically until you see a conversion, and then do the check described below. (Note: You don't need to wait until you get your first conversion before moving onto the next lesson!)

    Once you do see at least one conversion, check your tracker stats to confirm that you're seeing the same number of conversion(s) are showing up there too (in the "Conversions" column).

    If so, it means your postback url was set up correctly. Congratulations!

    But if you're seeing at least one conversion in the aff network stats, and not seeing any conversions in the tracker, it means you've set up postback incorrectly - either your postback url at the aff network is wrong, or you've appended the wrong tokens to the end of your offer link before adding it to the tracker. (Another possibility is if you've accidentally replace outgoing links on your lander with the actual offer/affiliate links, instead of your Voluum click url.)

    If you're seeing at least one conversion in BOTH the aff network stats AND the tracker, but the numbers are different, then there's either a delay in the aff network posting back to the tracker, or there are other issues at the aff network. Checking with your AM about this would be a good idea.



    *****

    Start a Campaign Journal

    It's important to develop good habits early on - and one of the best habits you can have, is keeping a Campaign Journal.

    I must be one of the laziest people, so I don't like doing anything that's not absolutely necessary, but I keep campaign journals. Why? Because when I'm running lots of campaigns, after a few days I wouldn't remember when I made which changes to which campaign.

    As a result, I would repeat certain tasks several times or forget to do others. You probably have a much better memory than I do, but trust me, once you have more than a few camps running at the same time, it WILL get confusing - and frustrating to keep track of everything in your head.

    Here is an example of a campaign journal spreadsheet you can download. Feel free to edit it to suit your needs:

    https://www.dropbox.com/s/tnye3khi7a...rnal.xlsx?dl=0



    You can start one of these files per month, and start one tab per traffic source.

    What you can do is start a row for each campaign you create, then insert a new column to the right of the "Campaign name" column for every day you check your stats. If you check and optimize your campaign multiple times a day, you can either create multiple columns for the day and include the time of day in the column heading along with the date, or just write several entries within the same cell, for example:

    8am - Cut placements 12345 and 67890, >2x payment in loss.
    2pm - Cut offer 2468 - reached stat sig [note: = statistical significance]
    9pm - Cut placement 54321, >2x payment in loss.
    Start a campaign journal RIGHT NOW, and enter the name of your first campaign in there.



    *****

    Update Campaign Costs in Voluum

    Costs passed back to the tracker from PropellerAds and PopAds are rarely accurate. However, we should appreciate the fact that these networks even pass costs to the tracker - many of the other networks don't.

    Because of the inaccuracy, it would be necessary to check the actual costs at the traffic networks, and manually update the campaign costs in Voluum.

    It's worth noting that even AFTER this manual cost adjustment, when you drill down to the various variables in Voluum - placements etc. - that the cost PER TRAFFIC SEGMENT will STILL not be correct. This is because we choose the Smart CPM option, which means traffic from different placements will have different rates. And there's no way for Voluum to know what these rates are.

    Having said that, doing this manual cost adjustment would still give you more accurate cost stats, so would be recommended.


    Manual Cost Updating - PropellerAds:

    -On PropellerAds' Dashboard, set the date range to include the days you've been running this camp:



    -Verify that the "Campaign" tab is highlighted, and make a note of how much your campaign has spent, in the "Cost" column:



    -Next, log into Voluum, click to select your campaign, right-click on the campaign and select "Update Cost":



    -Set the timezone to "(UTC-05:00) Eastern Time (US & Canada)" to match the timezone all PropellerAds stats are shown in, specify the date and time ranges as appropriate (basically starting from the date and time you started running traffic, up to the current hour in EST - it doesn't matter if you don't know/remember which hour traffic started running - just set the start date and time to something earlier than your actual start date and time and you should be fine). Then enter the cost you got from Propeller in the "Cost" field, and click "Save".



    Every time you want to check stats and make decisions based on cost or profits (for example when cutting placements), you'll need to update costs first.


    Manual Cost Updating - PopAds:

    -In PopAds, click "Report" in the sidebar. Leave "Template" at default (I don't think it matters), set "Date and Time Range" to "Lifetime" (in which case you won't have to change "Timezone" or "Week Start"). In "Additional Filtering" > "Campaigns", select your campaign and click "Add >>". In "Group and Ordering" set all settings to "Disabled". Click "Generate Report":



    -Follow the same procedure described above to update costs in Voluum.



    *****

    That's all for now!

    In the next lesson, we'll learn how to test offers and landers in detail!

    So just to shine more light on it (because there are so many different URLs you have to know about and set up properly I get it might be confusing in the beginning):
    • Once your campaign is successfully saved, a new Campaign URL is uniquely generated.
    • The campaign URL link has to be pasted on your traffic source's side.
    • The campaign URL redirects a user from an ad to a landing page through Voluum.
    • A click URL / multi-offer click URL is a link or set of links provided by Voluum that directs user from a landing page to an offer or offers.
    • The click URL (xxx.xx/click (without /1)) / multi-offer click URL (xxx.xx/click/1) must be pasted into the body section of the landing page HTML.

    Basically, if you browse directly to the lander, clicking on the outgoing links won't get you anywhere - because by doing so you'd be bypassing Voluum altogether.

    If you browse to the VOLUUM CAMPAIGN URL, Voluum would automatically REPLACE the lander links with the aff link, so that when you click on them, they would lead you to the right offer.

    And Voluum would know to rotate your landers and offers. Let's say you're running one lander and 2 offers. When you browse to the Voluum campaign url repeatedly, approximately half the time you'll get sent to offer 1 when you click on a lander link, and the other half you'll get sent to offer 2.

    On top of that, you can also check your error log in Voluum for invalid conversion request. This can give you more info on what's wrong with your postback.

    To view it, simply click the Error log tab.



    Then, drill down to see individual errors.



    If you submitted your postback URL to the affiliate network but conversions aren't being registered in Voluum, you'll most likely see Invalid conversion request error.

    Possible reasons: Incorrect {cid} token value or the {cid} token is missing from the postback URL.


    If there's nothing after "cid=" this might indicate that you forgot to append click ID to your offer link and there's no click ID value stored on your affiliate network's side to put under {clickid} token.

    If there's "cid={your_affiliate_network's_token_for_clickid}" , maybe the token you mapped with Voluum's cid parameter is incorrect and it's not what this particular platform uses.

    Show us the urls Amy mentioned, and we'll find the problem for you.
