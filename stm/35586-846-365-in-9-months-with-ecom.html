    My name is Sean from Seattle, just joined the community today. I've been doing ecommerce and the whole Shopify/dropshipping deal since August 2016, generating $846,365 across 3 different niche stores.

    First things first, a little more info about my Shopify stores:
    • All 3 of these are Aliexpress dropshipping stores using Shopify as the cart platform

    Goals for the remainder of 2017:
    • Build better branding around the stores + sourcing more unique products

    Thanks! Do you have any personal experience with viral sites or are you just basing on what you hear/see?

    As far as your ecommerce plans - your approach is one I wouldn't personally take but could work if you know the industry you are selling to really well. I never try to be the 'first mover' with a niche or product. Reason being is you don't really have anyone to model after, and I like to model what's already successful.

    It's also a better idea to define your target market FIRST - it sounds like you may have it a bit backwards with 'hoping' they find it interesting/useful and buy it.

    What you really should be doing is:
    1.) identifying a target market/niche (either one that is very passionate like a hobby/pet/politics, or one that solves a problem like acne or skin cream)
    2.) digging into your niche and figuring out their passions and pain points
    3.) finding solutions/products that can solve these pain points or that their passion would lead them to buy

    Another thing you need to think about is how you are going to target your niche market on FB. I don't know what your product is, but just from the sounds of it I would imagine that medical equipment would be difficult to target and sell on FB. FB is a social platform and the products that I've seen do best are ones that solve everyday problems, or cater to a passionate audience.

    Ecom is not about YOU, it's about your CUSTOMER and market . Assuming or 'hoping' someone will buy your product before doing any research into how to target them and if there is demand is a way to lose money quickly.

    Hope that helps!

    Great questions! I'm not an email expert so it could probably be done better - but here's what my setup looks like:

    I use Klaviyo for email btw.


    1. Abandoned Cart sequence
      • This is a 3-email sequence over 24 hours that is sent out to people who added a product(s) from my store to their cart but did not complete checkout (i.e. got distracted, went to another website, etc.). First email is just a "Hey you forgot this" type of reminder, and the other 2 are 10% and Free Shipping offered.

    2. New Customer sequence
      • This is a 5-email sequence that is more like a "welcome to the family" type thing, with a few upsells and other products from my store sprinkled into the emails

    3. Opt-in, but did not buy sequence
      • A kind of combination of #1 and #2 - sent if they did not buy. I have a Optin Monster email collection popup and it sends them this 3-email sequence welcoming them to the family and asking if they've looked around at our products

    4. Once a week 'new product' promotion
      • I will send an email blast once a week to my list of customers promoting a new product in my store


    I do not currently promote any other products on an affiliate or commission basis. Why only take a cut when you can have the whole pie it's the beauty of ecom.

    Hope that helps!

    Great questions!

    For starters, I am investing into a more evergreen brand/niche for ecommerce which will be my "main focus" (brain health supplements). My flagship product is a custom formula that's currently in production, and once it's ready my goal is to make it an 8-figure brand with 3 more products by mid-next year. I don't want to invest any more time into building up other niche stores that could sap my energy and steal my focus.

    Furthermore - this news site is around a passion I have and is completely outsourced in terms of the ads, social media posting, content curation, and article creation. If I can effectively monetize it, it will be a LOT more hands off and less time-consuming than building more niche stores. Maintenance is also a lot easier since there is no order fulfillment, customer service, payment processor issues, and significantly less time spent on setting up FB ads.

    If I could give one tip about people getting into the area - it would be to pick a niche that's PASSIONATE or that SOLVES A PROBLEM (ideally both) and research the shit out of it. Not just in terms of products, but also where the people in the niche hang out (reddit, forums, FB groups, etc), their 'lingo' or way of speaking, commonalities between people in the niche, their problems, etc. A lot of people take the general store approach but I think that's a mistake for people just starting out because of the lack of focus. If you live inside of a niche and learn everything about it, you will be able to sell them products they love.

    My biggest shock / time / money drain in ecommerce - customer service and dealing with things that I shouldn't be. I care about my customers - but this became a HUGE time/money drain because I cared TOO much. So much so that I took way longer to hire out than I should have, and was answering emails myself for way too long instead of growing the business. I probably lost out on a good 3-400k in revenue during Q4 because after I found a winning product, instead of finding more, I was answering emails! My advice is to get things rolling, get sales in, start talking to your customers in the beginning and handling customer service, but IMMEDIATELY start creating policy docs and automated email scripts/responses that you can hand off to a VA.

    Facebook groups and blogs I recommend - I'm in very few FB groups because there's just too much noise in ecommerce industry (as I'm sure you affiliates can relate). The only 2 that I'm in are Kingpinning and Trackify Group. I don't do too much reading, mostly just taking action and testing things on my own so I can't recommend any blog posts specifically. I learned from paying a 21-year old kid from Bulgaria $3000 to teach me the dropshipping ropes - safe to say it was a great investment lol. I do have a paid small group coaching program that has digital + live training over 4 weeks, but I'm not here to sell any of you on that.

    A lot of the stuff you want to learn can be found on youtube for free! Or if there's a shortlist of topics that STM members want me to cover, I would be more than happy to make a series of posts

    Hope that helps my friend!
