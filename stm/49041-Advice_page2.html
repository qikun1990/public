    Much helpful advice has already been given. But here's to help you learn "how to fish" for yourself in the future.

    To find out which geos and verticals are doing well, combine intel from several places and look for patterns:

    -Ask AMs on at least several affiliate networks for recommendations
    -Check spy tools to see which verticals are hot for any specific geo
    -Check with your affiliate friends if you have any, for any tips

    If you're not very experienced, stay away from tier 1/2 geos and go with tier 3/4 geos (i.e. choose developing countries, not developed countries). And choose offers with low payouts so you can test (and optimize) more offers for the same budget.

    To find out which landing pages work: In Adplexity apply your filters (geo, date, whatever else) and sort by "received most traffic". Then look through the top 50-100 landers and you'll see several very different styles. Rip 5 or more to start. Fix them up (yes as @twinaxe has suggested PLEASE LEARN SOME CODING!!) and test them. You may even want to test 2 variations of each different style to get a better idea on which style is best. Once you have a winner, test more lander variations for that style and even add your own completely unique lander. Or, just take that first winner and test more offers.

    You can't go far wrong by ripping landers that have received a lot of traffic over long'ish periods. Offers get saturated way more quickly than landers do - the landers that are working right now in pop and push look basically the same they did a few months ago. So test 5-10 landers first, lock down a good one, then put most of your focus on testing offers until you find one promising enough for you to then focus on campaign optimization.

    There isn't a one-fits-all method for deciding when to stop optimizing, but here is what I do personally right now :

    When I'm testing something a new batch of LPs, I put them into one campaign to do a balanced split test... if it's more than 5 or 6, I split them up into 2 campaigns etc. Take the numbers with a grain of salt though, I might run 7 in a single one, but definitely won't try to split test 15 LPs in a single campaign.

    I will pause those that fall behind quickly on the first day, sometimes within hours, depending on the volume and how "bad" they perform. I always try to pick at least 2 winners from any test, the more the better really.

    Once I have these winners, I give them some more traffic, to confirm they still perform well even on increased exposure.

    Now, if there are any profitable ones in the mix and the ROI is decent compared to what I'm usually seeing in a given vertical or niche, I stop the testing process and scale them as much as I can.

    If the ROI is negative or not high enough, I try to make several variations of the best performing LPs, in order to increase the ROI. Once I reach the stage I want to be at, I scale again.

    However, if I'm not able to reach positive ROI within a few days, I scratch these LPs and move on to the next batch. The amount of days depends on how well the LPs actually perform, I will dedicate more time to those that are close to breakeven or slightly positive...

    At what point do we stop testing and focus on a winner? How do we determine that?
    First find a offer that converts good enough to use it for testing landers.

    Set a new test budget then and use that offer to test different landers with it.

    When you have a winner = lander that converts best with the offer you can use it to test more offers for that targeting.

    should I just focus and make another 5 variations of the spin wheel?
    Before you create different variations of the same style it´s better to first focus on offers.

    An offer usually has much higher impact on your campaigns than a different variation of a LP style.

    In other words, how much testing is enough?
    There are no hard numbers about how much testing is enough but generally you can say that when you set a test budget and the lander doesn´t work on a good offer after the budget is spent then you mostly don´t need to continue with it.

    How will we make all the money back from testing all the variations and combos of landers?
    Test fast and effective and don´t waste money while testing.

    That way you keep the test cost as low as possible.

    Then you make the money back with campaigns that run long enough and have enough earnings potential to compensate the losses from the tests.

    And when we do finally find 1 or 2 winning landers, do we just invest hard onto those landers by purchasing the highest quality traffic?
    Not necessarily.

    You need to find a good balance between trraffic quality and price.

    It won´t get you anywhere when you only buy the highest quality traffic when you then pay expensive prices for lower volume.

    In the end you need to buy traffic that

    a) converts
    b) isn´t too expensive so that it´s worth it
    c) has enough volume

    In the 40-day tutorial I go into detail on how to do split-testing and determine a winner using statistical tools. For testing landers here's the method: https://stmforum.com/forum/showthrea...Banners-Part-1

    As for the exact test approach: Everyone does it differently - there's really no one mechanical method that will guarantee success. And there is never any promise that you will make your money back - ever. But the more experience you have, the more you can rely on your "gut feeling". And statistical tools help with the decision making as well.

    But your suggestion of making more variations of the winning style and testing them is definitely a viable method. Note that you can rip variations from Adplexity too.

    And each situation is different. For example, say you're doing initial testing of 5 landers with different styles and they all suck, then may be you should test more offers first before narrowing down landers. In that situation, dumping more money to find a "winner" and then testing variations of that "winner" would be silly. This is why I suggest to test a few offers from the start where possible - that way you can use an offer that converts reasonably well to generate enough conversions to cut landers down to a winner. Using a dud offer to cut landers would be unnecessarily (and even prohibitively) expensive.

    As for "highest quality traffic": You can of course experiment with that, but high-quality often means more expensive, so that's something you'll need to test to find the sweet spot.

    Yup - you'll develop your system as you gain experience.

    Taking your example of testing 8 offers with 4 landers - a few thoughts:

    -Make sure you're fairly confident that at least one of the landers is a good converter for the type of offer you're testing. On Adplexity - sorted by "received most traffic" over several days, lander styles you're seeing a lot towards the top - pick those and you can't go far wrong. Optimize them for speed. Then you can make the assumption that at least one of them will convert well.

    -As for offers: For initial testing try to stick to AM-recommended offers, although you can include ones that aren't.

    -Make sure your traffic quality is good, i.e. use a proven high-quality source (e.g. PropellerAds) and don't bid too too low. (Exception: You just want to bid the minimum possible and mass-test offers until you find something that converts even for low-quality traffic - that would be a viable approach as well. Depends on what you're trying to do.)

    -Run 10x payout ad spend to each offer (e.g. say the average offer payout is $2 then run 8 x $2 x 10 = $160).

    -You may not need to finish that $160 budget - if at any time one of the offers reaches 2 conversions (3 would be safer), pause the rest and just run that one offer to cut landers down to a winner. Then use that winning lander to retest the offers to cut THAT down to a winner.

    -If at any time it looks like the campaign is hopeless, i.e. there's not enough room for optimization to get you from current ROI to green ROI, stop running traffic. It takes experience to be able to tell this and most newbie WILL overrun a campaign - but better overrun and learn from stats than giving up too early and potentially miss out on the learning.

    -If after the $160 (in our example) you haven't made a single conversion, you should test another geo+vertical combination - because if your landers are good, traffic quality is good, and you've tested quite a few offers that were recommended by your AMs - and STILL not getting conversions or getting shitty conversion rate, then you'd likely have better luck with another geo+vertical than wasting money trying to make this one work.

    Hope that makes sense! And yup please feel free to discuss this further!
