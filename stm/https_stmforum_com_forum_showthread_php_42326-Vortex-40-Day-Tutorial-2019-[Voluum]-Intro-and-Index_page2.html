    Hi @alchamos! Welcome to the forum! And thank you for the kind comment!

    The method and principles are relevant, yes! But please keep in mind the following points:

    1)The various user interfaces - for aff networks, trackers, traffic networks, etc. etc. - have changed. They're always being updated, and at a speed way faster than I can keep up my updates of the tutorial. But it shouldn't be difficult to figure out how to do the same things in the updated interfaces - and if you're stuck on anything, make a post to ask.

    2)The competition in pop traffic has risen. There are still plenty of affiliates banking with pop - just not as many as a year or two ago.

    The more popular traffic type nowadays is push traffic. It's similar to pop, so it would be great to go through the 40-day tutorial first, run some pop, and THEN start testing push. With push traffic there's an additional variable - the ad. With pop all we do is use a landing page to convert visitors. With push, the visitor sees an ad, which they click on to arrive at a landing page.

    The 40-day tutorial will take you through all the basics, including tracking, landing pages, split-testing, and optimization. So no matter what type of traffic you decide to focus on later, it would serve as a good primer.

    Hope that helps!

    3. configured Voluum but for some reason it is only sending back clicks (from ad), clicks on prelander and leads from offer page (form fill). Leads from offer page show up as conversions on Voluum and conversions do now show up at all.
    Question: Can you advise?
    In Voluum, by default, any duplicated click IDs which are received will be automatically discarded to prevent false conversions from being recorded. So if you want to record both leads and conversions as conversion events in Voluum and also, be able to differentiate them, assign different payouts per conversion type, etc. you need to either add transaction parameter (txid) to the postback URL (easier setup) or use custom conversions (gives more advanced possibilites), whatever works best for you.

    You'll find more info on both here:

    Using transaction ID to track subsequent conversions

    Custom conversions

    About your questions.

    Is price the main reason to follow Binom vs Voluum? What are the pros/cons or reasons for a newbie to pick one vs the other?
    Well, Binom is selfhosted and comes with no additional cost no matter how much volume you run but you have to run it on your own server.
    But this is no problem, installation is very easy and the Binom support is very helpful and can also install it for you.
    Running and maintaining the server is also no problem, I have Binom running for about 3 years now and never had any problems.

    Voluum on the other hand is a cloud hosted tracker.
    That means that you don´t have to take care about the installation or so, it is installed on their own servers so there´s nothing to worry about for you.
    On Voluum you have limited events included.
    User that visits your tracking link, user that clicks through from lander to offer, user that converts and trigegrs postback are all events.
    The number of events depends on the plan you have.

    So in the end your decision should be if you don´t want to have it on your own server or not and how much traffic you run.

    I personally would say for pop traffic a selfhosted tracker is be a better match.
    For other traffic where you run CPC campaigns it´s a bit different because there usually not that much traffic reaches your tracker compared to pops.

    And especially as a beginner you will probably run pop campaigns in low tier countries where traffic is cheap, payouts are low and where is much volume avilable.

    With the high volume it´s pretty easy to reach the monthly events limit and with the low payouts you often pay less money when you use a selfhosted tracker because there you don´t have any overcharge.

    And then we have the third candidate in the ring, FunnelFlux.

    Basically, why is the option of Binom and FunnelFlex offered for this tutorial?
    When the 40 days tutorial was published first Voluum was by far the most used tracker and Binom wasn´t much known among international affiliates although it already had users in Russia.
    That´s why the original 40 days tutorial was only for Voluum.

    But the landscape changed a bit and other trackers also got more popular.

    More users started to use other trackers beside Voluum as well and the two of the most popular are Binom and Funnelflux.

    I already wrote a bit about Binom and Voluum above, let´s talk about Funnelflux as well.

    What makes FF different from basically all other trackers is it´s visual editor.
    On Binom, Voluum and other trackers you have the traditional way to set up campaigns whereas Funnelflux is pretty unique there as you can see here:



    FunnelfFlux also has some very good features that you don´t have anywhere else.

    Another advantage of FunnelFlux is that it offers both options, self hosted and cloud hosted, so the choice is up to you.

    I guess choosing the right tracker can be a very important decision for a beginner because usually when you found your tracker you will use it for many years.

    So before you rush with your decision just check them out.

    FunnelFlux offers a trial where you pay only $1 for the first month so that you can test it to see if it´s easy to use for you.

    Binom has a live demo that you can check HERE and when you want to test it you have 14 days free trial there.

    I am not sure if there is also a trial for Voluum but their support is very nice so when you want to check it just send them a PM HERE and I am sure there will be a way for you to check it out.

    Long story short, all three are good but I would say just check out Funnelflux because it offers both hosting options and also is somewhat one of a kind with its visual editor.

    It you've never run campaigns before, my advice would be to make minimum deposits to propellerads and go through the 40-day tutorial for pop first, then "graduate" to push.

    That way you can benefit from the tutorial and learn for cheap, because push traffic tends to require higher-payout offers than what you can run on pop, therefore more budget would be required to collect the same amount of information for you to practice analyzing and optimizing. Also, push traffic has one extra moving part: The push ad itself, whereas pop only has three moving parts - landing page, offer, and traffic.

    Since pop traffic tends to be cheaper and has fewer moving parts, what some people do is using pop traffic to nail down a good landing page + offer combination, then take that to push and test ads.

    Of course, if you don't mind figuring things out for yourself, you can always start directly with push and skip pop altogether. You can even try that for a while and see how you get on first. If you end up being confused and thinking "I need more hand holding", the 40-day tutorial will still be there waiting. But don't forget too that you can always start a post to ask questions - whether you're following that tutorial or not.
