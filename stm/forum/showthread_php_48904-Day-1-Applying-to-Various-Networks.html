    ***********************************

    EXPLANATION

    1)In this tutorial, we'll be running popunder/popover traffic - referred to as Pop Traffic in general - to CPA or cost-per-action offers.

    If you're not familiar with what pop ads are, just go to any torrent site and click around until a full-page ad appears out of nowhere to take over your screen. Some of the most popular pop ads are push notification subscriptions ("[website.com] would like to use your Notifications" with the "Allow" and "Block" buttons), antivirus ("you have x viruses on your machine - subscribe to our service / install our software to protect your machine now!", and sweeps ("congratulations - you've won an iphone 11!")

    These CPA offers can be obtained from affiliate networks. We can pop the offer page in the ad directly. Or we can pop a presell page (aka landing page) in the ad, and if the visitor clicks on a link on that page they'd be redirected to the offer page, where if they choose to sign up for the offer, we'll get paid a commission.

    Pop is the cheapest form of traffic you can buy, which makes it a good traffic type to learn the basics of media buying - such as tracking, landing pages, testing, and campaign optimization.

    Pop traffic is also the easiest to run, because there are fewer variables to test. No banners are involved, and there's minimal audience targeting. You either just send visitors to the offer page, or send them to a presell page first and then the offer page. Nice and simple.


    2)The affiliate networks you've applied to above will try to contact you! Keep an eye on your email and chat messengers, and pick up on phone calls - basically monitor all the different methods of contact you've left on the application forms.

    3)Some of the networks may want to have a chat with you before deciding on approving your account. Just be honest about your experience level - all the networks above are newbie-friendly.

    Main things to remember are:

    -Be professional,

    -Make it clear that you'll do your best to succeed and that you're not just dabbling,

    -Let them know you're a member of STM and following this tutorial, and that you can get help from this forum when you need to, and that 4)you'll abide by their terms of service.

    The top concerns of affiliate network reps are:

    -They want to know that their time spent with you will result in profits,

    -They don't have to do a lot of hand-holding, and

    -You won't do anything shady to get them into trouble (both with the affiliate network and with the advertisers a.k.a. the offer owners).

    4)After you're accepted by an affiliate network, ask your affiliate manager ("AM" for short) for their skype/telegram/whatsapp whatever and add them - if they haven't added you first.

    You can also log into your affiliate dashboard after getting approved - on some of the networks you'll find the contact info of your AM there as well.

    5)Remember to set up your payment information with your AMs so you can get paid later. It would also be good to ask how often will payment be made, what the payment threshold is (i.e. what amount you need to reach in order to get paid in the next payment cycle), and whether you need to request

    Originally Posted by popcash
    But do you think the affiliates will discriminate based on individuals that own or do not own company? And at what point do you reckon an affiliate marketer should actually start a company to run their activity?
    I haven't confirmed this with affiliate networks directly, but I believe affiliate networks will have more confidence in an affiliate if they have a company name and a website - makes them look more "legit". But as I've mentioned, once an affiliate has generate good revenue with at least one network, they shouldn't have too much trouble getting into the rest. And it's not hard to get into the first few networks as there are lots that are newbie-friendly and have good offers. So I wouldn't start a company JUST for the sole reason of being able to get into networks more easily - but that's just me.

    As for what time an affiliate should start a company: What it basically comes down to is whether the tax benefits (write-offs etc.) will outweigh the cost of the extra paperwork and trouble from setting up the company. Tax laws in each country are different, and it's also possible to set up the company in another country. It really would be best for each affiliate to consult with his/her accountant to get advice that is customized to their unique situation.

    You can run any offer you like from any network - it's just that when you're a newbie it would be easier to stick to the same networks as in the tutorial so you can get step-by-step instructions on how to set up tracking links etc.

    You can run tier 1/2 offers. The methodology is similar to running tier 3/4 offers.
