    Day 10: Setting Up a Campaign on PropellerAds - ACTION

    ***********************************

    ACTION

    Today we'll be setting up the campaign on PropellerAds to buy actual traffic from visitors!

    Once your PropellerAds account is approved, proceed to make your first deposit - just add the minimum amount of $100 - you can always add more funds later.

    To make your deposit, log into PropellerAds, click "Add Funds" and the rest is self-explanatory.


    Once you've made the deposit, set up the campaign as follows:

    1)If you've set up more than one campaign in the previous lesson, choose ONE of them to launch on PropellerAds today.

    2)Log into PropellerAds, click "Campaigns" from the left menu, click the "Create Campaign" button.

    3)For the "Campaign Name" field: Copy and paste the name of the campaign you set up in Voluum yesterday.

    4)For "Choose Advertising Format" select "Onclick (Popunder)".

    5)For "Pricing Model" specify "SmartCPM".

    6)For the "Target URL" field: Go to Voluum > Campaigns. Put your cursor on the campaign you created yesterday and right-click, then select "Copy URL" from the dropdown. Paste that into PropellerAds.



    7)Leave "Frequency / Capping" at enabled (by default). Set frequency to "One visitor will see your ad 1 times per 24 hours".

    8)For "Traffic Options" select "All Traffic". Uncheckmark "Include Broker traffic" and "Allow Anti-Adblock traffic".

    9)For "Automatic optimization" leave at the default "disabled", and leave "Target CPA" as it is.

    10)For "Countries": Start typing your target country, then select it from the dropdown that appears. For "CPM" put 1.00 for now - I'll make suggestions on how to bid later. Leave "Cities" alone.

    11)Set both "Daily Campaign Budget" and "Total Campaign Budget" to $10. (These are the minimum amounts allowed.)

    12)Leave the "Audience" settings at default (i.e. nothing selected).

    13)In the "Campaign Schedule" section:

    a)For "Select Timezone", the dates and times matrix under it, and "Set display period" - leave all at default. These will only be relevant if you want to specify which times of day and days of week you want traffic to run, which we won't worry about now.

    b)For "Ad delivery method" choose "Standard".

    14)In the "Targeting" section:

    a)For "Platform" select "Mobile".

    b)For "OS", specify operating systems that are accepted by your offer (consult your "Offers Spreadsheet"). The only 5 options to choose from for this field are:



    Choose as many of these as are accepted by your offer.

    (Note that Windows Mobile and Windows Phone are the same OS, and that RIM and Blackberry are also the same OS. You may see them referred to as either in offer descriptions.)

    c)For "Browser": If the offer only accepts certain browsers, leave "Include" selected and in the empty field type and choose each browser that is accepted. Alternatively, if the offer accepts traffic from all browsers except one or more, select "Exclude" and specify browsers that are not accepted. (Note: If you leave this field empty, you will get traffic from all browsers.)

    d)For "Browser language", leave empty for now to target all languages (we've already specified a country to target earlier which should be enough). Exception is if your offer explicitly asks to exclude certain languages, which is very rare.

    e)For "Connection type" select "3G / LTE" since you're promoting a 1-click or 2-click offer, which only accepts carrier traffic and not wifi.

    f)For "Mobile ISP" leave "Include" selected by default, then specify the mobile carriers that are accepted by your offer. If you don't see the carrier you want, do a search in google for the carrier name - sometimes it will have an older or newer name. Communications companies are always merging and demerging which will result in a name change.

    g)Leave "Zone limitation" empty.

    15)Checkmark "I declare and guarantee that my campaign meets the Quality Guidelines", and click on "Start Campaign".



    You've set up your very first campaign! Congratulations on reaching this milestone!

    I should tell you right now that your campaign may not result in profits (although it could!) Focus on learning and not earning in the beginning, and one day soon you'll hit on your first profits.

    This campaign will not receive traffic (i.e. PropellerAds won't start sending visitors to your tracker link) until a reviewer at PropellerAds has reviewed your campaign to make sure everything meets their guidelines, and approves it. According to the page here:

    https://help.propellerads.com/advert...w-ad-campaigns

    "The process can take from 20 minutes up to a few hours.

    During the public holidays it may take up to 12 hours."

    Hopefully by tomorrow your camp will have been approved and some traffic will have been sent so we'll have stats to look at together.



    ***********************************

    Exceeding the character limit has become a habit - hopefully you're not finding these lessons too wordy! This lesson turned out to be so long I've decided to just split it across 2 days - we can look at campaign stats briefly tomorrow as well.

    I'm wanting to explain every single thing in these early lessons, because I'm assuming you have ZERO experience. As we progress to future lessons I'll explain less and less, and let you think more for yourself and make your own decisions.

    Edit: Question: I was on Propeller and the cost is shown as $10.01, visits at 13,576. On Voluum, it shows as $8.83 and visits at 12,009. I have read about clickloss and if my math is right, the loss is about 11-12%? Is that normal and also about the difference of a dollar-ish in cost?
    Clickloss is normal. At 12% you're quite lucky even.

    And costs passed back from the traffic source will almost always be off. The only exception is zeropark+Voluum, and only because they're by the same company and therefore have access to each other's database.

    Like Justyna suggested, you can manually update your costs. But even then, your per-placement costs (or per-any-segment cost for that matter) could be inaccurate.

    Fortunately, with pop camps, it's not crucial to be very accurate when it comes to cutting placements etc. Basically I quickly compare the overall cost shown in the tracker, witht the actual overall cost at the traffic source, and figure out an approximate difference. Let's say the actual costs are 30% higher than what's shown in the tracker, I would just mentally add the 30% to any cost I see when looking at tracker stats (for that particular campaign).

    Alternatively, you can export placement cost data from the traffic network, export revenue data from the tracker, and use a spreadsheet to match them up. This is kind of overkill in most cases of pop camps IMO. But if that's the way you want to go, please do a search in the forum on "combine cost and revenue spreadsheet" to find a spreadsheet I've shared in the past.

    If you don't have that many placements to cut, you can also just open traffic source placement stats in one window, and tracker placement stats in another, sort both by descending impressions for example, and cross reference actual costs and actual revenus and making cutting decisions that way.

    But as was mentioned before, I wouldn't get too hung up on accuracy when cutting placements for pop camps. Focus on testing offers and landers (especially offers!) until you're relatively close to green, then cut the bigger ones of the unprofitable placements, while scaling to other networks - and milk profits before the camp or the offer dies.

    Efficiency will trump accuracy in most cases when it comes to pop. Speed of execution is of essence.

    Originally Posted by purdey67
    Hi Amy, thanks again for the awesome tutorial. I am running a Mobidea offer on Propellor Ads and it has had over 3000 views in about 12 hours but zero clicks. Should I pull it at this point?
    Zero clicks in the tracker? If so that's normal - because we're direct-linking, there will be no clicks registered in the tracker. "Clicks" refer to when visitors click on the landing page.

    If your traffic spend is excessive compared to the number of conversions, that's when you stop the campaign. I would suggest to stop if you've spent 10x the amount of offer payout and still haven't made a single conversion - that's like the worst case scenario. You may decide to cut an offer at 5x payout without a conversion even. It all depends on how much room there is for optimization.

    e.g. If a geo is small and there's not much traffic, then there's not a lot of room for you to cut placements etc. to reach profits. Plus the fact that we're direct-linking so you can't optimize by testing landers leaves us even less room to increase the ROI. So if an offer doesn't look like a winner right out of the gate, I would cut it. 5x payout without a conversion could be a good rule of thumb.

    Just in case: Are you seeing traffic recorded in your Mobidea stats though? As long as the offer you're promoting is getting traffic, then so far so good in terms of tracking.
