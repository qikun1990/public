    Day 1: Applying to Various Networks (Binom Version)

    ***********************************

    ACTION

    Let's start off with some simple preparatory tasks - like registering for accounts, and getting into the right mindset.



    1)First of all, I'd like to ask you to sign up to several affiliate networks that are newbie-friendly AND have a large variety of offers - which is perfect for beginners that haven't yet decided which type of offers (also known as an OFFER VERTICAL) to focus on.

    I'll also suggest some sample replies for when you're filling out the applications. My suggested replies are based on the assumption that you're completely new to paid traffic and affiliate marketing. If you already have some experience, then you'll know to fill-in accurate and truthful information that reflect your actual level of experience.




    a)Proceed to Mobidea and sign up for an account.

    Click the "JOIN / LOGIN" button at the top. On the next page click on the link in "Not registered yet? Do it here."

    First page is just some personal contact information - only fields marked with stars (*) are mandatory. The rest are optional. When finished click "Next".

    For "You are...", select "Media Buyer".

    For "Main traffic sources" enter "Pop traffic networks such as PropellerAds and PopAds."

    For "Monthly revenue" select "€100 - €500" if you're a total newbie. If you're making more, of course select accordingly.

    For "Which verticals do you work with?" checkmark "Games (CPA)", "Sweepstakes (CPA)", "Mobile Content (CPA)", "Videos (CPA)", and "Coupons & Vouchers (CPA)".

    For "Which type of models are you interested in?" select "CPA" and "CPL".

    For "Top 3 countries" enter "Kenya", "Greece", and "Argentina".

    For "What networks are you currently working with?" If you're already working with other affiliate networks, list them here. If you're not yet working with any other affiliate network, just reply "None yet - I'm a newbie following the 40-day pop tutorial on STM."

    The rest is straight forward. Note that you have the option of signing up with your google/gmail account if you have one - it would make signing into your Mobidea account in the future easier.




    b)Proceed to Clickdealer and sign up for an account as a publisher (not as an advertiser/agency).[/B]

    For "Account Name" enter the name you have associated with your bank account (either personal or company bank account you'd like the payments to be sent to).

    For "Your company URL", if you have a company website, enter the url here. If not, checkmark the "No website" box.

    For "Were you referred by ClickDealer AM or an active ClickDealer publisher?" select "yes". Then for "Please specify", reply "Vortex from STM". (Note: I never accept referral commissions - this is to let clickdealer know you're following this tutorial in order to increase your chances of getting accepted.)

    For "How did you hear about Clickdealer?", reply "STM Forum".

    For "Specialization" select "Affiliate/Media Buyer".

    For "Please name your top verticals" select "Mobile subscriptions", "Sweepstakes", and "Vouchers&Leadgen".

    For "Are there any specific offers or verticals you plan to launch with ClickDealer?", reply "Mobile subscriptions, Sweepstakes, and Vouchers&Leadgen".

    For "What CPA networks do you currently work with?", if you're already working with other affiliate networks, log into each of those networks to take screenshots that show your account name, volume for the previous month and names of the offers you’ve been sending traffic to. Upload the screenshots to imgur.com and paste the links here (one per network) - click "ADD LINK" if you have more than one). If you're not working with any other affiliate network, just reply "None yet - I'm a newbie following the 40-day pop tutorial on STM."

    For "How much monthly revenue do you generate with other networks?" select "< 1k".

    For "What traffic types do you generally use for promotional purposes?" select "Pop/Redirect".

    For "What are the top 3 countries you promote offers in?", reply "Tier 3 and 4 countries".

    For "Please include any other information that ClickDealer should know about you or your company", this is a good opportunity to tell them a bit about yourself in order to increase your chances of getting accepted! Your affiliate marketing experiences, any other online/off-line marketing experiences, non marketing-related work experiences, life experiences...basically anything to help them see you mean business and have what it takes to succeed.

    All other fields are self-explanatory. Click "Finish" to submit the application.




    c)Proceed to Gotzha and sign up for an account as an affiliate (not as an advertiser).[/B]

    For "How did you hear about Gotzha?", reply "Forum".

    For "Which vertical(s) do you run?", reply "mobile content, sweeps".

    For "What are your top offers (name some brands)?", reply "None yet - I'm a newbie following the 40-day pop tutorial on STM."

    For "What are your top countries?", reply "Tier 3 and 4 countries".

    For "How much monthly revenue do you generate with other networks?", select "$0 - $1000".

    For "What is your traffic source?", reply "Pop traffic networks such as PopAds and PropellerAds".

    For "Your Company Name" either put your company name if you have one, or your own name if you're operating as a sole proprietor / individual.

    For "Your Company Address" either put your company address if you have a company, or your home address.

    For "Website", no need to put anything. If you have a company website feel free to put it there.

    For the "Financial information" section - this is not mandatory. You can specify your payment information later in order to receive payments.

    All other fields are self-explanatory. Click "Finish" to submit the application. Click "Submit" to submit the application.



    2)Sign up for accounts on 2 pop traffic networks. These are places we'll be buying the visitors, aka "paid traffic", from.




    a)Sign up for an account at PropellerAds.

    Click on "Register" at the top and choose to sign up as "Advertiser". We need to sign up as an "Advertiser" and not a "Publisher" - this is true when signing up for any traffic network. (However, when signing up with affiliate networks, we need to sign up as a "Publisher" and not "Advertiser". Confusing, but two sides of the same coin.)

    First page is just some personal contact information - only fields marked with stars (*) are mandatory. The rest are optional. When finished click "Next".

    For "Please describe your business model" select "I am an affiliate and want to promote products from CPA networks or direct advertisers".

    For "What other traffic sources and CPA networks do you currently work with?" put "PopAds, Mobidea, Clickdealer, Gotzha".

    For "What advertising formats are you planning to use?" select "OnClick (Popunder)".

    For "What verticals and market niches do you currently work with?" enter and select "Sweepstakes".

    For "Comments" enter "Run pop traffic to legit mainstream CPA offers from affiliate networks such as Clickdealer.com. I'm following the 40-day pop tutorial on STM."

    Accept the terms and conditions and click on "Sign up".




    b)Sign up for an account at PopAds.

    Click on "Sign-up".

    For "Account Type" select "Advertiser". The rest of the fields are self-explanatory.





    3)Learn how this forum works! Click into the various forum sections and subsections to check them out. Also read this post on how to contact STM if (God forbid) you ever get locked out of the forums (and save this information somewhere!)





    4)Consider getting a tracker subscription at Binom.org. This is software that will show us detailed statistics about our campaign, and allow us to control where to send visitors.

    Why did I say "consider"? Please proceed to the "EXPLANATION" section below - I have advice for you on how to pick a tracker.

    Please make that decision and get a tracker before proceeding with the rest of this tutorial. This tutorial has / will have 3 versions, one for Voluum, one for Binom (this one), and one for Funnelflux. Please follow the respective version after deciding on a tracker. You'll find all 3 versions here:

    https://stmforum.com/forum/forumdisp...l-2019-Version



    5)Sign up for a Binom account.

    And be sure to take advantage of the discount we've arranged for you here:

    https://stmforum.com/forum/showthrea...or-STM-Members

    Please also check the rest of the Discounts subforum for any other discounts/promo that Binom may be running currently:

    https://stmforum.com/forum/forumdisp...mber-Discounts



    6)Sign up for hosting for Binom.

    (Obviously, only proceed with this step if you've decided to choose Binom as your tracker.)

    Next, you'll need to get a server to install Binom on (as it's a self-hosted tracker).

    You can find server requirements on this page:

    https://docs.binom.org/install.php

    If you're not sure which server to choose, here are some recommendations from Binom:

    https://docs.binom.org/faq.php ("The server choice" > "Which server would you advise?")

    If you're not sure how much traffic you'll be running, I would suggest to pick a server for either 500,000 clicks/day or 700,000 clicks/day. Those should be more than enough for completing this tutorial, and if you need to run more traffic afterwards, you can always upgrade your server.





    7)Register a custom domain for Binom.

    Next, you'll need to register a domain, and point it to your server.

    Because I haven't used any of the webhosts recommended by Binom, I won't be able to provide a complete step-by-step on how to do this - but I'll do my best and trust you to figure out the rest!

    Also: Binom's support is very responsive and helpful. Here's their skype if you need their help: binom.support

    Back to registering a domain - I use Namecheap as the registrar, but you can use any other registrar - if you do, you'll need to adapt the steps below to your particular registrar.

    -Register an account at Namecheap.com if you don't yet have one.

    -Sign in, click on "Domains" > "Domain Name Search".

    -Think of a domain name for your Binom domain. Something generic-sounding such as tracking12345.com will do. It doesn't matter much - your visitors won't be seeing this domain name. (In fact, if you have a spare domain lying around, you can just use that.)

    -Enter your desired domain name into the search field and press ENTER. Domain names that have already been taken will show a "Make Offer" link at the end of the row. Domain names that are available will have a price and an "add to cart" button at the end of the row.



    And no, it doesn't have to have a .com extension. .xyz, .club, .life domains are cheaper for example, so would make good choices.

    -Once you've decided on a domain name, click on the shopping cart icon, then click on "View Cart" and go through the remaining purchase process to buy the domain.

    Purchasing for 1 year would be enough (in case your domain gets banned by google before then).

    Getting WhoisGuard is recommended, as many pop campaigns are at least somewhat shady in nature so the privacy could be a layer of protection - and the fact that it's free for the first year (at the time of writing) makes it a no-brainer.

    No need to get Premium DNS or any other service they try to push at you.

    -Once you've registered the domain, on your Namecheap dashboard go to "Domain List". Find your new domain and click on the "Manage" button for this domain.

    -This next step will vary depending on your webhost - we'll need to point your new domain at your server. Below are the 2 methods on how to do this - you'll need to find out from your webhost which method is necessary for your server. The information should be included in the welcome letter from your webhost, but if it isn't, submit a support ticket to your webhost to ask.

    To be absolutely clear: You'll only need to execute either step A or B below depending on your webhost and server.


    A)Point domain to server via A record.

    In namecheap, you should now be on the settings page for your new domain, and in the "Domain" tab.

    Find the "Nameservers" section and make sure it's set to "Namecheap BasicDNS", which it should be by default:



    Click on the "Advanced DNS" tab. Under the "Host Records" section, there should be 2 records. Modify them as follows, substituting IP and domain name values with your own (you'll need to get the IP from your webhost):



    Click the green checkmarks at the end of each row to save the values.


    B)Point domain to server via nameservers.

    In namecheap, in the "Domain" tab in your new domain's settings, find the "Nameservers" section and set it to "Custom DNS". Paste the nameservers provided by your webhost:



    Click the green check mark to save.


    You'll need to wait up to 24 hours for propagation to finish. To find out whether it has or not, you can use a DNS checker tool such as this one:

    https://intodns.com/

    If the results state that your domain is pointing to either the IP or the nameservers (whichever method you used) you've specified in the Namecheap settings, then propagation has completed.



    8)Install Binom.

    You'll find detailed installation instructions here:

    https://docs.binom.org/install.php

    I would also recommend for SSL to be installed and turned on. Please see instructions here to make that happen:

    https://docs.binom.org/domains-ssl.php

    Again, if you encounter problems or have questions, hit up Binom support.

    EXPLANATION

    Just a couple of things I'd like to bring to your attention.



    1)When applying to networks, the verticals and countries you specify will not actually limit you to running only offers in the specific verticals+countries. Your answers may help your AMs to match you with some initial offers you run, but even for those you can ask for recommendations on any type of offer once you've established communication on skype etc.



    2)This one is important - I want to talk about choosing a tracking solution. I know I said I would try to take the decision-making out of the equation, but unfortunately this is one of the ones you'll need to make for yourself.

    This tutorial was originally written around the Voluum tracker, and subsequently adapted for use with Funnelflux and Binom trackers.

    I have yet to do a detailed comparison of these 3 trackers, but in a nutshell, here are some important points I want you to keep in mind when deciding on which tracker to use:

    -Price-wise, Voluum and Binom are the most economical.

    -Voluum is a cloud-based tracker, so that no matter where your target visitors are, the speed with which the tracker can redirect your visitors will not be affected.

    Whereas Funnelflux and Binom are self-hosted trackers that are each installed on a server - so depending on the distance between the server's location and your visitor's location, the redirection speed can vary. You can get multiple servers in multiple geographical locations and install a tracking on each, but each additional license would result in additional costs.

    This difference in redirection speed can have effects on your campaign's performance. I can't tell you how much, because that would depend on a plethora of factors. But I can tell you that it's not likely to make or break your campaign.

    -Voluum is managed, meaning all the hosting and maintenance is taken care of for you, so all you'd need to do is just sign up and start using. Binom is self-hosted, so you'd need to look into getting a server. Funnelflux has both options: Managed plans where hosting and installation are handled for you, and self-hosted plans.

    -All managed plans will specify how much traffic each plan can handle, and the more traffic it allows, the higher the cost. So when comparing trackers, do take this limit into consideration. For self-hosted plans, you'll need to choose a server that can handle the amount of traffic you're planning to run. Obviously, the more traffic you need to run, the more server resources you'll need, and the higher server costs will be.

    -The 3 trackers I've mentioned are not the only ones on the market. To start your tracker research you can browse our tracker subforum here, where you'll find threads that talk about the 3 trackers I've mentioned plus many more:

    https://stmforum.com/forum/forumdisp...king-Campaigns

    I would advise that you pick a tracker you can find lots of recent threads on this forum about, because that means many people will be able to provide guidance when you have questions in the future.

    Lastly: Whichever tracking solution you choose, if you choose a managed plan, pick the smallest, least-expensive plan. You probably won't be running very high traffic from the beginning, and when you do, you can always upgrade your plan.

    I'll leave this with you!

    And that's it for today. Think that was too easy? Don't worry - we'll get to the harder stuff real soon!


    In the meantime:

    The networks you've applied to above will try to contact you! Keep an eye on your email and chat messengers, and pick up on phone calls - basically monitor all the different methods of contact you've left on the application forms.

    Some of the networks may want to have a chat with you before deciding on approving your account. Just be honest about your experience level - all the networks above are newbie-friendly.

    Main things to remember are 1)be professional, 2)make it clear that you'll do your best to succeed and that you're not just dabbling, 3)you're a member of STM and following this tutorial, and that you can get help from this forum when you need to, and that 4)you'll abide by their terms of service.

    The top concerns of affiliate network reps are: 1)they want to know that their time spent with you will result in profits, 2)they don't have to do a lot of hand-holding, and 3)you won't do anything shady to get them into trouble (both with the affiliate network and with the advertisers a.k.a. the offer owners).

    After you're approved into an affiliate network, ask your affiliate manager ("AM" for short) for their skype/telegram/whatsapp whatever and add them - but it's also possible that they may have already added you in order to conduct the interview.

    Also log into your affiliate dashboard after getting approved - on some of the networks you'll find the contact info of your AM there as well.


    (UPDATE: Please note this heads-up from member sheet731:

    https://stmforum.com/forum/showthrea...l=1#post357761

    To improve your chances of getting accepted by Clickdealer, let them know you're following the 40-day pop tutorial on STM. If you still don't get approved, no big deal - you can always contact them again after you've run a few offers with the other network(s)!

    Don't be afraid to get rejected - you'll get that in this business. Figure out ways to improve and then try again. None of the decisions are personal!)
