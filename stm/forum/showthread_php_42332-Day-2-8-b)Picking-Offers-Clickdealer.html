    Day 2-8: b)Picking Offers - Clickdealer

    ***********************************

    ACTION

    In the previous lesson, we picked and applied to some offers on Mobidea, waited for approval, then picked one to run later.

    In this lesson, we'll do the same thing - but this time for Clickdealer. Clickdealer has been around for years and have stellar reputation. They have a large variety of offers in various verticals.

    If you haven't gotten approved by Clickdealer yet, feel free to skip to the next lesson - the one on Gotzha.

    (As was mentioned before, as a MINIMUM, you only need to be approved for ONE offer from ONE affiliate network in order to proceed with this tutorial. So don't worry if you can't do all 3 aff networks for now.)

    Without further ado - let's pick out an offer on Clickdealer!






    1)First off, we need to find an offer. I would like for you to apply to offers in 2 different ways for the sake of practice.

    a)The first method is to just browse offers and apply to one.

    -Log into Clickdealer. In the left menu under "Offers" click on "Browse".

    -Click on "Show Filter", then "Extended Search" to bring up more filter options. Set "Vertical" to "Mobile Content", "Payout" -> "max" to "2", for "Flow" select "DCB (1-click flow)" and "DCB (2-click flow)", for "Adult traffic" select "No". Click "Apply" to filter results.

    -If you put your cursor on the offer names you'll see screenshots of the offer pages. Just pick any offer that looks interesting to you, that accepts traffic from a developing country.

    b)The second method involves hitting up your Clickdealer AM (affiliate manager) on skype to ask for an offer recommendation.

    Ask him/her nicely to suggest a well-converting offer that fits these criteria:

    -1/2-click

    -Mainstream

    -Tier 3/4 geo

    And once they recommend an offer, they will likely approve it for you. But just in case - here's how you would apply for a clickdealer offer:

    -Log into the Clickdealer dashboard, and in the left menu under "Offers" click on "Browse".

    -Type either the offer ID or name or both into the search box and hit Enter. Locate the offer and click on the offer name to trigger this popup:



    -After clicking "Apply for offer" you'll get another popup:



    -For "Media Type" select "Pop/Redirect", leave the "Notes" field empty, and click "Apply". (For future reference: Most affiliate managers won't even check these answers - so basically just do the minimum here and apply for the offer - on some networks if you can't find "pop" in the dropdown, just pick any other type of traffic. It's not important.)


    2)Ask your AM to approve the recommended offer.

    No need to ask for the offer you picked out in 1a to be approved - that was just for practice. Just make sure the offer that was recommended by your AM gets approved before proceeding to the next step.


    3)Record offer details into spreadsheet.

    -Back in your Clickdealer account, go to "Offers" -> "Browse" -> do a search to find your approved offer. The "Status" column of the offer should say "Active" (if not, then your AM hasn't approved it yet - follow-up until they do). Click on the offer name to bring up the offer details.

    Remember how we logged the Mobidea offer details into that spreadsheet? Please do the same thing for this Clickdealer offer!





    To get the offer link: Scroll down the offer details page to "GET TRACKING LINKS", choose a "Creative" (most offer only have one offer page but some have multiple to choose from - just choose any) and a "Domain" (the one that will show up in your offer link - does not matter which), click "Copy URL to clipboard", and paste that into the spreadsheet.





    ***********************************

    EXPLANATION


    In this lesson we learned 2 ways to pick offers. I want to go into more details on these plus other ways you can use to pick offers.


    a)Asking AMs for best offers should be one of the primary ways to pick offers to run.

    AMs has access to offer stats in the backend that will tell them how many affiliates have run / are running each offer, and how well they're converting. So they can be instrumental in guiding you to good offers. Also, there are offers that are "capped", where the advertiser (offer owner) only accepts a certain number of conversions a day, that only select affiliates are allowed to run - usually those that are "tight" with the AM and have made good money with/for the network.

    This is why maintaining a good relationship with your AM can really help you succeed in your business. Entire posts can be written on how to nurture this relationship, but for starters, keeping the following in mind will help you:

    -Be polite but persistent. Many AMs are overworked, so they may not get back to you right away after you hit them up. Be patient. Give them a day or so to get back to you. But don't give up - follow-up daily until you get what you need. However, if you're finding that waiting more than a couple days at a time for a response becomes the norm, it may be time to work with a more newbie-friendly network!

    -Respect their time. Keep conversations brief and don't take up more of their time than you have to. Don't ask them to hold your hand - their job is NOT to teach you how tracking works or how to solve coding issues for your landing pages (although some WILL give you insight on vertical selection, landing page angles, and even which traffic sources has good volume for certain geos)!

    -Give them good ROI on their time. This is probably the SINGLEMOST EFFECTIVE thing you can do to get more of your AM's time and attention. Run at least some of the offers that are recommended to you and approved for you, before shiny-object syndrome sets in. Run bigger volumes as soon as you're able. Some of the bigger affiliates would even run campaigns at break-even or slight loss just to maintain a stellar relationship with their AM, in order to be included for the next big offer! Don't worry about running volume right now because you're new - just something to keep in mind for the future.


    b)Another way to find good offers would be to check out the affiliate network's "Top Offers Lists", which some networks would send via email, on a regular or irregular basis.

    Here's an example of a top offers list I've received from Clickdealer in the past:




    c)Another way is what we've done above: Just log into an affiliate network, browse the offers, and pick some to run.

    In our practice above, I asked you to just pick a random offer - because we're just doing direct-linking as an exercise and not aiming to make profits.

    But what if we were running an offer "for real", with an actual landing page? How would we know which offers to pick, other than ask for recommendations from the AM?

    First of all, I would recommend that if you're running in a new geo and offer vertical (offer vertical = type of offer, e.g. sweepstakes, gambling, antivirus, adult dating), that you start with AM-recommended offers. That way, chances are the recommended offers will give you the conversions you need to split-test landing pages until you end up with a good one.

    But once you have this benchmark offer+lander, you'll be able to test any offer you want!

    To clarify: You won't want to test random offers in the beginning when you don't have a proven lander - because then if you don't get conversions, you wouldn't be able to tell whether your offer sucks, or your lander sucks, or if they BOTH suck. But if you've already used an AM-recommended offer to test landers, and picked a winning lander, you could THEN use that proven lander to test random offers - because then if you don't get (let's say) at least 1 conversion after spending the equivalent of 10x the payout, you'd know the offer's probably a dud. Hope that makes sense!

    So once you have a proven landing page, you can just browse the offers from your affiliate network dashboard, identify the ones in the same vertical and geo you've been running, and test them using your proven lander.

    Of course, you could just ask your AM "what other offers can I test for vertical X and geo Y?" That would work as well.

    And even for offers you've identified yourself through browsing all available offers, you should still check with your AM on whether they are still ACTIVE (some may have expired) and are not CONVERTING BADLY. You want to just test active offers that are either converting well for other affiliates, or new ones that nobody has gotten around to testing yet.

    Browsing offers in this way will also allow you to see what kinds of stuff is available - the different types of offers/verticals, different geos, different conversion flows, etc. This can give you inspiration on what else you can test next.


    d)Last method to find good offers: Do some research.

    Look around and ask around!

    -You can start right here on the STM forum! There are members asking for offer recommendations all the time - you can view the replies to see recommendations from other members. You can also browse recent follow-alongs to see which verticals and geos they're having success with, and try them out yourself. You can also start a thread to ask for recommendations (please don't just ask something general like "what would be good offers to run?", but something like "which networks has good sweepstakes offers for South Africa?"

    -You can subscribe to one or more spy tools - no need to do that now as I'll be asking you to do that in a future lesson.

    Spy tools can tell you which offers from which affiliate networks are doing the most volume, what the hottest offer verticals are for a particular geo (and what the hottest geos are for a given vertical), which traffic networks are used, etc.

    -You can do manual spying, aka "spying in the wild". Basically just go to websites that have the kinds of ads you're planning to buy (for example, if you want to find pop ads, browse to some of the torrent sites), use a VPN to change your IP to the geo you're planning on running offers for, then click around to trigger various ads and take notes. Do this enough and you'll have a pretty good idea of what kinds of offers people are running, and the kinds of landing pages they're using.

    READING

    Some AMs do refer to 1-click offers as SOI. SOI stands for single opt-in and can include offers that collect name and email etc., without requiring the visitor to confirm their information (those are called DOI or double opt-in offers).

    The ultimate goal here is we're looking for offers that can convert when DIRECT-LINKED, i.e. without using a landing page (or what AMs refer to as "pre-landers"). I want to get you guys running your first campaigns in the easiest way possible to get that confidence going, and direct-linking is the easiest way. We'll build on that later by learning how to use landing pages. Offers that can convert without using a landing page, tend to be ones with the easiest flows - and 1-click offers fit that bill perfectly. The less action required by the visitor, the better the chances of it converting without a landing page. If the offer requires too much action from the visitor - even some offers that only ask for name and email - you'll likely need to use a landing page to presell them and motivate them to complete the offer.

    Hope I haven't confused you further! Basically if you ask your AM for offers with simple flow that have a good chance of converting with direct-linking, and if your AM knows their stuff, they should be able to recommend what you need.
