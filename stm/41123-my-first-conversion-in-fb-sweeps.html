    My First conversion in FB Sweeps

    Hi,

    First of all I am very happy with this forum and the attitude of people here, that only by sharing our knowledge and working together we can achieve great results.

    I have been running sweeps (Win H&M coupon) for past 3 days, it is my first campaign and wanted to have some feedback from you guys.

    Geo: DE
    Payout: 2.7USD
    Type: SOI (email submit)
    Traffic: Facebook (mobile only), single image
    Targeting: Women 35+
    Period: July 3 - Still running (July 5)
    Ad structure:
    I was reading Mation and Stickupkid posts (Thanks a lof for them) about about ad structure in Facebook and used same methodology, 1 campaign, 3 Adsets, in each adset 3 images.
    So I have tested 9 images and after 24 hours I have removed some of the iamges to lower the CPC, CPC Today 0.18$ (is it a good result?).
    My Prelander CTR - 42%, I had 68 visits to final offer and only 1 of them converted.

    Report from FB:

    Is It normal to have only one offer conversion after 68 visits to final offer and spent 55$ or it is too early to decide something?

    I would be happy to hear your comments, Thanks

    Although they are all similar fashion stores, you can't treat the stores on a par for targeting and assume you target right. As you might know a lot of women have a certain preference for stores/brands.

    If you play around too much with your campaigns, the higher chances are you will be manually reviewed.

    As @stickupkid stated, FB is not fond at all of sweepstakes. If your LP is non-aggresive, some reviewers might let it slide.

    A couple of things that have high chances of triggering a manual review are:

    • user reports
    • ad set duplication
    • running split tests with many different variants at once
    • increasing to a high daily budget
    • reaching a certain ad spend total on the ad account (like 10k)


    You can make them not ban you for your LP with a proper cloaking setup though.

    You might want to keep trying, but you will be fighting a lot just to get a campaign running if you don't have any resources for new ad accounts.
