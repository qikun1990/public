    Yes! You're practically almost breaking even without optimization - that's pretty darn good.

    No need to overthink the blacklisting of placements. Basically:

    -You can cut aggressively if there's a LOT of traffic and you're bleeding out your a** and need to optimize quickly. In this case, cut at 1x payout in loss, or even 0.5x payout in loss.

    But then when you get green, you may want to unpause some of those (gradually, in batches, to avoid cutting too much into your profits) for a retest.

    Or, if you decide to test more offers/landers and find a better-converting combination - may want to retest some of those placements again.

    -You can cut less aggressively if there isn't a lot of traffic available in the first place. 2x payout in loss should be good enough to cut a placement.


    Pop traffic is so volatile, I wouldn't spend time worrying about cutting in accordance with statistics and check for stat sig etc. Those rules of thumb have served me well, but everyone has different rules of thumb. You're encouraged to start with those, then do your own experimentation once you have more data.
