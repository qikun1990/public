    Day 3: Setting Up Tracking

    **************************************************

    ACTION

    Today we'll be setting up the Voluum tracker.

    A tracker will detect and display all kinds of stats about your visitors to help you optimize your campaigns. It will also allow you to specify how and where you want to send your visitors (e.g. directly to the offer, or to a landing page first and THEN to the offer), and will let you split-test multiple offers and landing pages.

    Let's get right to it!


    1)Log into Voluum. Click on "Affiliate networks" > "+ New affiliate network" > "Create custom" > in the "Affiliate network name" field type "Mobidea" > leave everything at default and click "Save".


    2)Still in Voluum, go to Settings (the gear icon at the top), "Tracking URLs" tab, scroll down to the "Postback URL" section, and copy the "Postback URL":




    3)Log into Mobidea. Put the cursor on the gear icon (Settings) at the top, then click on "Global postback", select "Custom", paste the Postback URL you've copied from Voluum into the "Insert your domain" field.

    Don't save it yet! We need to make 3 changes to the Postback URL:

    a)Replace "cid=REPLACE" with "cid={{EXTERNAL_ID}}".

    b)Replace "payout=OPTIONAL" with "payout={{MONEY}}".

    c)Delete '&txid=OPTIONAL".

    So the original postback and final postback url would look something like:

    http://track.yourdomain.com/postback...&txid=OPTIONAL

    http://track.yourdomain.com/postback?cid={{EXTERNAL_ID}}&payout={{MONEY}}

    Lastly: Scroll down, answer the security question, and click "Save Changes".




    4)Go to the Offers Spreadsheet you compiled yesterday. Copy the first link in the "Original Offer Link" column and paste it into the "Final Offer Link" in the same row. The link should look like this:

    http://www.rotationurls.com/offer/12...nt={sub_subID}

    Now for the link in the "Final Offer Link", replace:

    data1=Track1&data2=Track2&tag={External_ID_from_tr affic_source}

    With:

    data4={campaign.id}&data5={trafficsource.id}&tag={ clickid}
    (IMPORTANT: Please take out the space between the start bracket and the "clickid" - it's not supposed to be there. This is a vBulletin glitch.)

    And delete this part:

    &website={subID}&placement={sub_subID}

    So that the final offer link would look like:

    http://www.rotationurls.com/offer/12...&tag={clickid}

    Copy this link.


    5)Go back to Voluum, click on "Offers" > "+ New Offer". Paste the final offer link you've just copied, into the "Offer URL" field.


    6)Now we fill in the rest of the offer information in Voluum, by copying from the Offers Spreadsheet.

    Copy the "Offer ID and Name" name from the spreadsheet, go to Voluum, paste into the "Offer Name" field.

    Specify "Country" as the country your offer will accept, 'Affiliate network" as "Mobidea", and hit "Save".


    7)In Voluum, go to "Traffic sources" > "+ New traffic source" > "Trusted Traffic Source Templates" > select "PropellerAds", click "Load template", and click "Save".


    8)Still in Voluum, create new campaign:

    -Click on "Campaigns" > "+ New campaign". (Or, if you happen to have also signed up to Voluum DSP, then click on "Campaigns" > "+ New campaign" > "New TRK campaign".)

    -Assign a campaign name. You'll develop your own campaign-naming convention later no doubt. For now, just name it in the format:

    [2-letter country code] Main DL [Carrier]

    The "Main" stands for "Mainstream", which means non-adult. The "DL" stands for direct-linking. As for 2-letter country codes, you'll find them here:

    http://www.worldatlas.com/aatlas/ctycodes.htm

    For example, if your offer accepts traffic from Brazil TIM, name your campaign "BR Main DL TIM". If the offer accepts multiple carriers, just pick one.

    -Specify "Traffic source" as "PropellerAds".

    -Specify "Country" as your chosen country.

    -For "Destination" select "Path", checkmark the "Direct linking" box > select your offer in the "Offer" dropdown > click "Save".

    -A "Campaign URL" will appear at the top of the campaign window. Let's finish for today - tomorrow we'll be copying this campaign url and pasting it into PropellerAds to set up a campaign there to obtain traffic (i.e. buy visitors).



    **************************************************

    EXPLANATION

    So why do we need to use a tracker? The main uses of a tracker are as follows:


    1)Detect details about your visitors, and aggregate and display this data.

    Voluum can detect details of each visitor, such as their IP, country, operating system, device/brand/model, carrier, connection type (3g vs. wifi), browser...etc. This will help you to optimize a campaign - e.g. if a particular OS is losing you money but the other OSs are turning a profit, then you would stop buying visitors from the unprofitable OS.

    You will learn how to optimize a campaign in later lessons.


    2)Control the flow of your traffic, i.e. where to send which visitor.

    When creating a campaign in Voluum, you can specify where you want to send your visitors. For example you could send them directly to the offer page. Or send them to a landing page (aka lander, pre-lander, presell page, or bridge page) to presell them first, and when they click on a button on the landing page, send them to the offer page to increase the chances that they would subscribe to the offer.

    Without knowing all this data about your visitors and conversions, you'd be flying blind, as it would make it very difficult to optimize your campaigns.

    You could also specify multiple offers and/or landing pages to split-test them, to find out which one is the best, and then pause the inferior ones. You could also set up Voluum Rules to redirect visitors, aka "traffic", based on certain conditions. For example, you can set up Voluum Rules to redirect all Android traffic to one offer and all IOS traffic to another other, and the remaining traffic to a third offer.

    You will learn about landing pages and tracker rules in detail in later lessons. For now, just know that using a tracker is almost mandatory if you want to make money in this industry, which is why I've asked you to get used to using a tracker from the start.


    *****

    Next, I would like to explain some of the steps we've taken to set up tracking.

    The gist of the setup is:

    -We grabbed the offer link from Mobidea and put that into Voluum.
    -We created a campaign in Voluum, and specified direct-linking to the offer.
    -We took the postback url from Voluum and put it into Mobidea.
    -In the next lesson we'll take the campaign url and paste it into PropellerAds.

    So this is what will happen:

    -PropellerAds will send visitors to the Voluum campaign url.
    -Voluum will then take over, and send visitors to the offer we specified in campaign settings.
    -If a visitor ends up subscribing to the offer, aka "converting", Mobidea will record this, and "tell" Voluum about the conversion, by triggering the postback url to send that conversion data to Voluum. Voluum will then display the conversion in its stats.

    As for how we modified the offer link from Original to Final: Remember that Voluum will send visitors to the Final Offer Link you added to Voluum (when you were adding "+ New Offer"). Let's say the Final Offer Link was this:

    http://www.rotationurls.com/offer/12...&tag={clickid}

    {campaign.id}, {trafficsource.id}, and {clickid} are Voluum tokens. When Voluum triggers this link to send the visitor to the offer page, it will automatically replace these with actual values. And because this is a Mobidea offer link, Mobidea will read these values into its database and store them.

    So to clarify: Voluum will trigger the offer link as follows for example (replacing the tokens with values):

    http://www.rotationurls.com/offer/12...tag=12345abcde

    And Mobidea will store this visitor's data as a new row in its stats database. Imagine a spreadsheet with columns that say "data1", "data2", and "tag". Each visit will be logged in a new row. For this particular example, the value "1234567890" would be logged for variable "data1", the value "0987654321" for variable "data2", and the value "12345abcde" for "tag".

    {campaign.id} is a random string of characters created by Voluum to represent the new Voluum campaign you've just created.
    {trafficsource.id} is a random string of characters created by Voluum to represent the traffic source you've specified for this campaign.
    {clickid} is a random string of characters created by Voluum to represent the visitor - it's almost like a name they assign to the visitor.

    As for how we set up the postback on Mobidea: Remember we got the Postback URL from Voluum, pasted that into Mobidea and modified it to:

    http://track.yourdomain.com/postback...yout={{MONEY}}

    Whenever a conversion happens, Mobidea will trigger this url to "tell" Voluum that a conversion has been made. Mobidea will automatically insert the 2 values into the link:

    http://track.yourdomain.com/postback...de&payout=0.66

    The "12345abcde" comes from the database, where the {clickid} was stored. The payout of the offer, Mobidea would of course know as well.

    So, effectively, by triggering this postback url, Mobidea would be telling Voluum that "hey! Visitor 12345abcde has converted with a payout of 0.66!"

    And Voluum will then add this conversion to its stats.


    *****

    So why did we want to send {campaign.id} and {trafficsource.id} to Mobidea's database?

    After visitors convert, the owners of the offers will try to monetize them to make back the commissions they paid to the affiliate network for each lead.

    The offer owner won't be able to make back the money they paid for every single lead. Let's take gaming subscription offers for example:

    Imagine if we were using landing pages (these will be introduced in later lessons - for now, just know that a landing page is a pre-sell page that we show to visitors, before sending them to the actual offer page, to increase the likelihood that they would subscribe to the offer). If on the landing page we lie by telling the visitors that the game is free to play forever, then the conversion rate would be higher.

    However, after the user subscribes to the offer, they may be enraged to discover on their next phone bill, that the game isn't free after all. What do they do? They cancel - likely before the offer owner has made back their investment.

    This type of leads will yield low life-time-value for the offer owner, and are therefore referred to leads with "bad lead quality". Whereas leads that will provide high life-time-value, have "good lead quality".

    And being misleading while pre-selling is only one of the factors that can influence lead quality. The visitors you buy from certain traffic sources can result in better or worse lead quality than other traffic sources. For example, leads from FB traffic will usually be of better quality than leads from pop traffic. In turn, not all pop sources have the same traffic quality.

    So why do we need to care about lead quality? Why not leave that for the offer owner to worry about?

    Answer: Offer owners are not stupid. If they can't make their money back from the leads we send them, they're not going to keep paying for leads from us.

    They would ask the affiliate network to tell us to stop promoting the offer.

    This is where sending the Campaign ID and Traffic Source ID to the affiliate network may potentially help us. This information gives the affiliate network and the offer owner the option of telling us which specific campaigns and/or traffic sources are responsible for the bad-quality leads, so that we'd have the option of stopping those campaigns or stop buying from individual traffic sources, and continue running the remaining campaigns. If we don't send the Campaign ID or Traffic Source ID, then when our lead quality is deemed unacceptable, the only solution would be for us to stop promoting the offer altogether, i.e. get kicked from the offer.


    *****

    Lastly - you may be wondering why we picked Voluum as our tracker?

    I picked Voluum because it's the tracker I know best. It's also the simplest to get started with because it's cloud-based as opposed to self-hosted, where you'd need to get a server to host the tracker, figure out how to install the tracker and how to optimize the database and the server for smooth operation, and set up multiple installations in multiple geo-locations to minimize tracker installation speed. (The closer your tracker installation is to your target country, the faster the tracking redirection speed. For users on mobile devices, slow speed will make you lose visitors.)

    Having said that - Voluum is definitely not the cheapest option once you start running higher traffic volume, which is easy to do with pop traffic. So if keeping operation costs low becomes a priority for you later on, do consider FunnelFlux, which is a very versatile tracking system with lots of awesome features. It's a self-hosted solution though (but I have it on good authority that a cloud-based version is in the works!)

    Let me chime in too a bit

    We are creating campaigns at two places : at the traffic source and in a tracker.

    1. In a traffic source - it's a good idea to create a new campaign for every segment that has very different bids. SO carrier traffic VS wifi, iOS VS Android, specific very large placements VS RON ... otherwise you'd be overpaying for some segments, while paying to low for others, which would result in very mixed traffic quality and wasted $$$.

    2. In a traffic source - since you can rotate multiple LPs and Offers in one campaign, technically you could use one tracking campaign to handle traffic from several campaigns at various traffic sources.

    BUT, you need to realize that trackers distribute traffic to LPs and offers evenly, so for example ... 1st click to 1st offer, 2nd click to 2nd offer, 3rd click to 3rd offer ... then 4th click to 1st offer again ... So in theory, if you'd send traffic from 3 sources to this "example" campaign, it's possible that clicks from source 1 would only go to offer 1, clicks from source 2 would only go to offer 2 ... assuming that all sources would send same amount of traffic and in the same order, which is quite unlikely, but still possible.

    Because of this, you need to also create separate campaigns at the tracker, in order to properly test different traffic types or targeting groups. Otherwise it will all get mixed up and only very high volume will help you to get actionable data.

    I personally usually do a separate campaign at the tracker for every campaign that I create at a traffic source. It means a lot of campaigns at times, but it's also the easiest to analyze the data this way.

    As promised, I've tested the Mobidea postback with Funnelflux.

    In Funnelflux, set up Mobidea as a new offer source like this:



    Then take the postback url on that same screen, go to Mobidea > Settings (little gear icon at the top) > Global postback. Select "Custom" and paste the postback URL into the text field. It should look something like this:

    http://yourfunnelfluxdomain.com/tracking/conversions/postback.php?flux_payout={{MONEY}}&flux_txid=&flux _hid={{EXTERNAL_ID}}

    Next - copy the offer link from Mobidea. Let's say the original offer link from Mobidea is like this:

    http://www.rotationurls.com/offer/12345|54321?data1=Track1&data2=Track2&tag={Externa l_ID_from_traffic_source}

    You would put the following link into Funnelflux when adding a new offer:

    http://www.rotationurls.com/offer/12345|54321?data1={campaign-id}&data2={trafficsource-id}&tag={hit-id}

    Yup - the payout will be posted back automatically as well.

    Basically, all you need to do is tell Mobidea that you're 1)using Voluum, and 2)what your Voluum subdomain is. And they'll know to do the rest - including appending "&tag={clickid}" to the end of all your affiliate links.
