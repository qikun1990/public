    How I Made $XXX,XXX Profit On FB Direct Linking (Blackhat But No Cloaking)

    Some of you may already read some comments at the Facebook vs Pin Submit discussions about Facebook Advertising for VIP Response. I have been doing FB advertising (self-employed) for over 5 years now, mainly on insurances and finance. Facebook seems to be a tough b*tch when it comes to pin submits. However I would like you guys to show some insights on how to make good profit on FB with pin submits. Some luck is needed too by the way ;-)

    Important notes v1.
    – no cloaking involved
    – no prelanders involved
    – just direct linking (ad → click → campaign page)

    So, what do we need to start;
    – a FB account, warmed up and war ready!
    – branded pin submit campaign, in my case mainly UK, IE and AU. For example Tesco or Marks & Spencer

    Setting up the ad
    Create a new Facebook fan-page, relevant to the brand. This part makes the ad thrustworthy and is key to make the campaign profitable. For example; TescoShopping. Could be risky yes, but very much needed.

    First, I come up with a good title/header, around 7 words, to catch the attention of the consumer. For example; Why Not Compete For a €500 Tesco Voucher? It doesn't have to be agressive, just a plain simple header could do the job. Without much misleading the spam reports, bad comments etc will be less too, what keeps your ad healthy and profitable for a while.



    Second, Google as much as possible images/photo's regarding Tesco (instore, shopping bags, front store etc). Trust me, even the dumbest looking images could have the highest CTR! Test these images all with the same line (maybe not the best way, but for sure the easiest way to see which images work best).

    Third, come up with a good call to action, it's not needed to be agressive again. “Just Answer the Question” will do perfectly for example.
    Last, use a visible url, something like; tesco-vouchers.co.uk/enter-here

    Targeting
    Let's not make this very hard; women 35+, who are interested in Tesco (similar brands)
    Mobile newsfeed only.

    Now your campaign is live, keep your ads clean from bad comments, block any “spam, cost, enter, subsciption” words at the FB page settings. Keep some doubting comments, to make it thrustworthy and not too one-sided.

    And ofcourse I don't have to tell you to optimize on text/image/audience/targeting during the campaign to get the best CTR, lowest cpc and best ROI!

    Some insights
    – over 100% ROI on average
    – CTR' of 7% on ads are reached, on broad audience (3 mill+)
    – trying to find the right angle is sometimes easier than you think
    – again, an image which doesn't attract you, might attract your potential audience (so not only test images which you “like”)
