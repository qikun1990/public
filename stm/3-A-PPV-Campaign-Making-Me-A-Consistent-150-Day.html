    A PPV Campaign Making Me A Consistent $150+ /Day

    Here's my first giveaway!
    I was hesitant to give this one away because it was a longterm campaign of mine! ..but it's so easy to get started and so cheap i had to show it to you guys so you can start making money quick without hurting your bank accounts! It's a great one too because it can be scaled horizontally, so you all can take this one and start making some money!

    Quick definitions:
    Scaled Vertically: Taking a campaign and scaling it by using the same idea.
    ie: growing traffic sources
    Scaled Horizontally: Taking a campaign and scaling it by multiplying the idea.
    ie: taking a campaign your doing with cameras, and scaling it by moving it to printers as well

    So this campaign has made me a min of $150 a day for the last few months and i've only run it on TrafficVance.
    The campaign idea is creating a custom question people can vote on, and displaying an email submit as the prize. I'm going to use my Justin Bieber campaign as the example since it yielded the highest profits.

    Some days i spent $4 and made $40!!!
    1000% ROI! (just with this bieber campaign)

    My Campaign Idea
    Was Justin Bieber Discovered on Youtube?
    [YES] or [NO]
    *Get a 2011 iPhone 4*

    My PPV Targets
    I targeted EVERYWHERE that was Justin Bieber related.
    - Do a URL scrape and find all just bieber targets.

    Some scraping ideas:
    - Justin bieber photos, Justin bieber lyrics, Justin bieber youtube, Justin bieber myspace, Justin bieber tickets, Justin bieber concert…. leave nothing out. An extra hour of targeting will be the difference between $4/day ad spend and $8/day ad spend, which is either $40/day or $80/day!

    My Landing Page



    Scaling It Horizontally!
    This is the real beauty behind this campaign idea. It's 100% ENDLESS! I've tried some celebs who didn't profit at all, but most do, and some profit BIG! You don't just have to do celebs either, you can do "fake personalities" such as dwight from "the office", or chefs, or brands, or pets. The more creative you are here the more profit you will make per campaign and the longer your campaigns will last!

    Matching Your Offers With Your Idea
    Remember who your targeting demographic is and match the appropriate email submit. If your targeting Justin Bieber, then your demo is girls 13-20, so make sure your email submit is a pair of ugg boots, not a free dinner at Red Lobster.

    Scaling Vertically!
    This is always the beauty of PPV! Once you get a campaign or 2 profiting on 1 PPV source, move it to another. I highly recommend Traffic Vance, MediaTraffic and CPVDirect. (Let me know if you need a referral!)

    Another Angle To Take
    I've been successful promoting this campaign idea with a battle landing page.

    Battle landing page example:
    Who Has Sold More CDs?
    Justin Bieber or Miley Cyrus?
    *This way you can target twice the amount of URLs and sometimes it yields better returns because people are passionate about "their" celebrity!

    Take this campaign and scale it first horizontally! Then Vertically!
    Ask any questions you have here, enjoy!

    Keep from getting scrubbed with email submits:

    1.
    Originally Posted by kyleirwin
    Switch networks, get multiple accounts from the network you're working with, or just build a profitable enough campaign so that even when the epc tanks, you'll still remain profitable
    2. I do batches of network split tests. Say the offer is email submit for Nike.
    - I'll take the offer across 5 networks, and have them rotate through a script.
    - 2 days later i'll change my landing page to a best buy email submit, and do the same thing as step 1 with the best buy submit
    - 2 days later i'll do it with a new offer, ie: 52" Vizio TV
    - then you can keep doing this, and eventually go back to your first offer and repeat.
    It's a giant repeat process, but it takes 30 seconds a day to switch your landing page/offer url.

    You can even build a script to have them all switch out every 48 hours. (I haven't done this, but seems like a good idea!)

    Hey Twonickle,

    OK lets get started:
    1. I do submit sometimes, but have had this campaign running for a while! Sometimes i have to pause it, but most of the time it's profiting $100+ a day.

    2. This is the beauty of this campaign type! As i mentioned in the original post I'm targeting, celebs and brands mostly, but you can do anyone or anything that is known by a certain niche. IE: Doing the same justin bieber style landing page with "GOKU" from Dragon Ball Z, will likely yield the same results as long as your targeting dragonball Z. So in the end the offer itself doesn't matter too much as long as it relates to people who watch dragonball Z. So A bestbuy gift card, a 52" plasma TV, and a iPhone 4 all are things no Dragonball Z fan would turn down. Submits are usually things people want, they aren't to niche specific so its really easy to rotate them with a campaign like this.

    Just a tip, I've been running these campaigns for a while, the goal is to send quality traffic still otherwise i would have been booted. So stick with legit topics. Dragonball Z might not be the best converting traffic for an advertiser so i wouldn't recommend it. I just thought it was a good example niche.

    3. I usually keep rotating. Once the initial burst of leads has come in and all the offers settle, they usually convert within 1-2% of eachother. Keeping them rotating every 2 days is pretty important. Makes the campaign last longer.

    4. The testing of new campaigns itself is quite easy, usually the niches are small and at max spend $15 a day. That's with 1000's of URLS. So i test for 2 days, spend a max of $30 (i just can't get anymore traffic than that since the campaigns are so niche targeted) and see what i yield. Half the time i can tell just after one day.

    I also test with only 1-2 submits. Usually 1 submit. It's not enough traffic for a single day test with multiple submits.
