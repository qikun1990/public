    Taking my Facebook+Sweeps virginity

    Hey STM, Lyhras the Noobie here!

    I've run a few offers on Facebook before, with limited success (read: not in the green). I'm determined to change that now though, and as such, I'm starting this follow-along. I'm a big fan of writing down your thoughts and actions as you learn 5x as much by analyzing what you did, and why you did it. That's the quickest way to progress and truly learn from your mistakes and successes. Aaaand of course I'm also hoping a few of you guys will pitch in and throw a golden nugget or two my way

    After having a bit of a wank over a few stickupkid posts, I've decided to try my hand at running sweeps on Facebook. This was actually my plan anyway before joining STM last week, so finding so much great information on this topic was a very pleasant surprise.

    I'm going to stick pretty closely to the various threads by stickupkid on the topic of sweeps. From there, I hope to make my own experiences and develop my own way of doing things.


    Setup overview:

    GEO: Nordics
    Traffic Source: Facebook
    Vertical: Sweepstakes
    Tracker: Voluum
    Payout: 4€


    Long term goals:

    I have a long term goal, of being able to launch 1 new campaign per day. I also have a goal of getting to $1k/day before the end of 2018, and at that point begin to outsource technical parts of the business. That leaves me to take care of higher level jobs (angles, copywriting, systems, etc)


    Goals for this campaign:

    1. Develop and refine a system that works for me in getting campaigns up and running in an effective manner
    2. Learn more about the optimization process, and make a system to effectively optimize.
    3. Become more familiar with diving into data.
    4. Getting to a steady 50%+ profit/day.
    5. Not get my ad account suspended


    Facebook setup:

    - Testing women 30+, targeting interests of various supermarkets.
    - Running 1 campaign, 3-5 adsets (split-testing angles), 3-5 ads (only splittesting images in each adset, not copy)
    - Mobile Newsfeed
    - Automatic bidding
    - Budget/adset: $10/day
    - Sending clicks to a survey style prelander


    Next actions (pre-launch):

    - Fix the rest of the lander. There's currently a JavaScript checking the location of the user, and as I only know very basic HTML, I have no clue how to get around it. At the moment my biz partner has the page (he's a programmer) and it'll hopefully be ready to go soon.
    - Create and setup a fanpage related to the offer. I need a profile pic, banner and a short text about the page.
    - Brainstorm 5 angles for the ads, and find 3-5 suitable images to test with
    - Setup everything in Voluum
    - Put the page online, and check everything's running smoothly

    Hopefully getting most of this done tomorrow.


    Questions for the pros:
    - Would it make sense to test PPE vs CTW right away? I'm thinking of starting out with CTW to eliminate 1 variable, and depending on the performance I might test it up against PPE later.
    - If Facebook decides to bend me over and take me to Brown Town (ie shut down my account), will it only affect that particular ad account, or my entire business manager?
    - How important is it for me to "warm up" a new Facebook page? Should I start running a few soft ads on it, or is it only necessary to warm up on an ad account level?


    P.S.

    For anyone interested in my background and how I got started, you can check out my "Introduce Yourself" thread here

    TL;DR
    Been doing bulk email for 1.5 years for a company, have a small marketing agency doing Facebook marketing, so I'm pretty familiar with Facebook. Now trying to combine AM and FB.

    - Testing women 30+, targeting interests of various supermarkets.

    I prefer to focus on the supermarket voucher you are pushing only up front (if audience is broad enough to start with ofcourse) and not combine with other supermarket brands. Lots of consumers really only go to ALDI for example, so targeting other supermarkets would mess up your precize targeting, which may end up with a high cpc/cpm.

    If you pinpoint to the one brand only, and your ads perform bad, you know the creative/angle isn't attractive enough, since your targeting is on point. Ofcourse it could be the audiences are saturated with these offers but that's not very likely in Norway.

    - CTW is the only thing I do, so I can't answer the PPE alternative honestly.

    - FB probably only kill the current account and not the rest, you are running on one BA now or on the personal account?
    - Keep the FB page a bit lively and get some traction will def help out for better placements/cheaper clicks at the end, but then we are talking about lots of traction to see significant improvements. I barely touch the FB pages to be honest, just keep my ads clean from too many complaints and that's it. But I don't recommend this if you rely on only couple of accounts.

    At last, be careful with scaling, adjusting ads etc, keep your ads on low budget for a while. Just to learn but also to keep FB away as much as possible from your account!
    Good luck for now!

    Hi there, I am no pro and I am new to STM forum but can offer my advice to your inquiry. I have ran successful sweeps campaigns on FB for about 1 year and took some to $xx,xxx total revenue in total. I would say the best advice to keeping a healthy fb account would be seasoning your fb ad accounts, pages and personal fb profile.

    When I farm:

    I create a profile under my real name. I fill out every piece of the profile as I can. I start to organically start liking pages and videos as you would normally. I let the account season for a few weeks to a month sometimes longer. Then I open a business account, again with my real name and fill out all details. Then I open my page(s) the same day as opening FB BM and again fill out as much info as possible. I fill out every single aspect of the page that is possible. I will even post relevant images and videos and post long posts with relevant hashtags and emoji's. Sometimes I will schedule posts most the time I won't. Then I will wait about 1-2 weeks before launching my first ad and when I do I start with $5.00 a day PPE ad for a day or two days then increase the ad to $10 and launch another $5.00 and continue increasing day by day. Once I have been billed a few times and my limit increases I will start launching bigger budget and different campaign objective ads (Conversion ads).

    This has been process for all my ad accounts that I have run sweeps on and I have yet to have an ad account shutdown. I have had ad accounts shutdown for scaling to fast, but i have since learned my lesson. Also, im sure this forum goes into discussion about angles, but what has worked the best for me with sweeps is making sure your angle for the sweeps offer is not too direct, but making sure that you use copy that still has intent for your offer... i may be getting off topic now lol

    Hope this helps! Also, let me add, after I have seasoned my bm fb ad account(s) i literally can create a fb page (filling out all the details as possible) and launch an offer on it the same day.

    Day 4+5, 23-24.03.2018

    Campaign finally launched. These are the stats for the day 1 of the live campaign.

    Revenue: 108€
    Spend: 52,29€
    Profit: 55,71€
    ROI: +106,54%

    More than 100% ROI on day 1 of the campaign is better than I had dared to hope for! Seems I might be able to make this work after all

    These are the stats for day 2 (up til around 6pm).

    Revenue: 32€
    Spend: 20,48€
    Profit: 11,52€
    ROI: +56,25%

    Not quite as good as Day 1, but still pretty decent!

    I want to dive a bit deeper into the campaign itself. This is from the start of the campaign until 6pm day 2.

    Offer:

    Clicks: 259
    Conversions: 32
    CR: 12,3%

    I'd been told the page converts at around 30% by my AM. But seeing as I'm in the green already, I guess I can live with 12%.

    I'd probably have to pre-frame the offer better to get a higher CR. Maybe by making my lander more in the style of the offer page, more people would convert.

    Lander

    These numbers are based on what Facebook reports, along with the affiliate network.

    Visits: 432
    Clickthroughs: 259
    CTR: 59,9%

    A 60% CTR? Yes, please! The lander is a simple survey style with 4 questions, testimonials, and an autoredirect after all questions has been answered. So I guess it's not THAT surprising. With a CTR this good, would it make sense to start splittesting with another lander?

    As I see it, I could do it two ways.

    Lander 2.1: New questions and/or new testimonials that pre-frames the offer better.
    Lander 2.2: Make it more in the style of the offer page with layout, colors, etc.

    Honestly, I'm pretty satisfied with the landers results, and feel like my efforts are better spent elsewhere.

    Ads

    I splittested 4 angles, and after 36 hours, I have a clear winner.

    (The currency used here is Danish Kroner, for anyone wondering)



    Translated into Euros:

    Adset 1: CPC - 0,105€
    Adset 2: CPC - 0,244€
    Adset 3: CPC - 0,291€
    Adset 4: CPC - 0,206€

    I've turned off adset 2, 3 and 4, so only Angle 1 is running right now.

    This is what it looks like inside adset 1.



    3 of the ads haven't really had a lot of exposure, but image A1-D and A1-A are performing really well. 10%+ CTR, and a very healthy CPC of 0,1€ and 0,09€.

    I think the move here will be to turn of images B, C, and E and let the other 2 run. Maybe find a few other images to test.

    I could maybe try to find similar images to the winning ones, and see if I can find anything that performs better.


    What's next?

    I don't want to start testing too much, as I still don't have a ton of data to base my assumptions on. I was maybe even too quick with my actions already?

    I think I'm going to let it run for the rest of the weekend, and accumulate more data.

    But here's my thought's of the next possible actions.

    1. Start scaling. I can copy this campaign to my personal ad account, and run the winning angle with a higher budget.
    2. Start testing a larger audience. Right now, the audience is only around 110k, which is not a lot if I want to scale. I could test a broader audience (no interest targeting) and reach an audience of around 1.3M.
    3. Test with other supermarkets. I could try and test with other supermarkets, and see if they convert just as well.
    4. Refine this campaign. I could adjust and test more ads and landers, and try to squeeze as much ROI as possible before I start expanding and scaling.

    As I said, it's probably too early to make any decisions before I have more data to work with.

    Any feedback would be much appreciated!

    Day 7, Monday 26.03.2018

    These are the stats for the last 2 days (up until 3pm Monday)

    Revenue: 88€
    Spend: 19,07€
    Profit: 68,93€
    ROI: +361%

    My CPC has decreased significantly on my 2 winning ads. It is now around 0,05€, so half that of my cheapest ad a few days ago. Ad CTR hovering around 12-13%, and RS of 10.

    After mulling things over for a bit, this is my plan of attack.

    1. Start split-testing new audiences.
    There are about 10 major retailers that I can target, which will bring me to a total audience of 500-600k. I'm going to create an adset with each of these targeted as an interest. I'll tag the link, so I can see in Voluum which interests convert. I'm also going to create an audience with no interest targeting (only demographic), and exclude the supermarkets as interests. That way I can see if it will convert.

    I'm going to use my winning copy and images, along with a few new images in the style of the winning images.

    2. Duplicate my winning adset 2 times.
    My spend is currently 13€/day (100 Danish Kroner) on my winning adset. This brings in a revenue of 40-60€/day (if the trend continues). I think I can safely duplicate it a few times without taking notice by Facebook and risking a banned account.

    3. Expand to other countries.
    I can't bring this exact campaign to other countries, but I'm going to try a similar campaign in a similar country. As the countries are different, I'm going to start "from scratch" with my original 4 angles, just to test if they resonate with something else.

    Side note:
    Currently trying to prepare myself mentally for not-green-right-off-the-bat campaigns.

    It's easy to be motivated when things are going well, and a campaign is in the green. I've been lucky to hit a green campaign on my first try with sweeps. Right now, I'm trying to learn as much as I can, as that is a better investment than going for pure profit right now. Aiming to learn will be more beneficial when I inevitably hit a bad campaign (or one that's not green right away).
